Homemade Classic Chili

Let's learn how to make the king of manslop meals, a bowl of Chili! so easy that even a science based lifter could make one with little to no obstacles! Whether it's meeting your macro goals or just being too lazy to make something that your girlfriend would like, this classic all american recipe well be sure to get the results you desire.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb of lean ground beef
  • 2 cans of red kindey beans
  • 1 cup of beef broth
  • 1 can of crush tomatoes
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 2 cloves of minced garlic
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 1 jalapeno, diced
  • Chili Powder, to taste
  • 1 tbsp of cumin, or to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions

  1. Preheat a dutch oven pot over the stove and medium-high heat. While it's preheating, we'll prep the other ingredients
  2. Dice the onion and chop the bell pepper and jalapeno to have them prepped and ready. Once cut up, set aside for later.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, mix the ground beef with the chili powder, salt, and pepper. Make sure that all those seasoning are mixed well before putting it the dutch oven. Brown the beef in the dutch oven. Drain the grease when done.
  4. Once the beef is browned, dump the diced onion and chopped bell pepper and saute the vegetables with beef for around 4-5 minutes or until the onions are golden.
  5. Dump the beans, canned tomatoes, and beef broth in with the meat and vegetables. Add the jalapeno, chili powder, and cumin then stir. Season to taste with salt.
  6. Bring the pot to a boil then reduce to a simmer. Let it simmer for about 30 minutes. If a thicker chili is desired, sit for closer to 45 minutes.
  7. Top with shredded cheese or add frito chips for a frito pie. Yum!
